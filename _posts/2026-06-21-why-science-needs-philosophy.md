## Introduction

Science succeeds because it limits itself to disciplined questions. Philosophy helps explain those limits, clarify the concepts being used, and examine assumptions that cannot be tested by a single experiment.

## Main Discussion

Every scientific practice contains philosophical commitments. A measurement assumes a relation between instrument, world, and interpretation. A model assumes that selected variables are enough for the question at hand. A theory assumes standards of explanation.

This does not make science less rigorous. It makes rigor more visible. Philosophy contributes by sharpening concepts such as cause, probability, law, explanation, evidence, reduction, emergence, and objectivity.

Statistics makes this visible. A simple likelihood expression,

$$
P(D \mid H),
$$

does not decide by itself what counts as a good hypothesis. It must be placed inside a broader account of inference.
