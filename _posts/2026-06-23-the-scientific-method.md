## Introduction

There is no single mechanical recipe called the scientific method. There are recurring practices: careful observation, model construction, prediction, experiment, criticism, replication, and revision.

## Main Discussion

Science becomes powerful when it binds imagination to constraint. A hypothesis can be bold, but it must expose itself to possible correction. A model can simplify, but it must say what it leaves out.

Physics often illustrates the method clearly. Newtonian mechanics predicts motion through equations such as:

$$
F = ma
$$

The equation is simple, but its power depends on definitions, measurement procedures, idealizations, and domains of validity.
