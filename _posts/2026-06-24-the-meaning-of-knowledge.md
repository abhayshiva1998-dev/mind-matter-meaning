## Introduction

Knowledge is more than information. A person can possess many claims without understanding their grounds, limits, or consequences.

## Main Discussion

Classical epistemology often begins with belief, truth, and justification. But these categories become richer when placed in intellectual life. To know responsibly is to know how evidence was obtained, what alternatives remain, and when confidence should change.

Logic helps preserve clarity. A valid inference has a form that protects truth if its premises are true. For example:

$$
\forall x (Human(x) \rightarrow Mortal(x))
$$

This kind of structure does not settle all questions, but it prevents many confusions from entering unnoticed.
