## Introduction

Literature does not replace science or philosophy. It answers a different need: the need to preserve the texture of lived experience, ambiguity, memory, desire, conflict, and moral attention.

## Main Discussion

A novel can show what an argument only names. It can hold contradictory motives in view without resolving them too quickly. This makes literature a discipline of attention.

Literary meaning is not arbitrary. It emerges from form, diction, genre, historical context, and readerly judgment. Interpretation is therefore neither pure subjectivity nor mechanical decoding.
